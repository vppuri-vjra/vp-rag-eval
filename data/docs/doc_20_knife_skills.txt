TOPIC: Knife Skills — Basic Cuts

Consistent knife cuts ensure even cooking — pieces of the same size cook at the same rate. Mastering basic cuts is foundational to professional cooking.

THE CLAW GRIP:
The most important safety technique. Curl fingertips under, knuckles forward. The flat side of the blade rests against the knuckles, guiding the cut. Fingertips never protrude past the knuckles. This prevents cuts even if the knife slips.

BASIC CUTS (from largest to smallest):

1. LARGE DICE: ¾ inch cubes
   - For stews, soups, roasted vegetables

2. MEDIUM DICE: ½ inch cubes
   - Most common dice, salads, salsas

3. SMALL DICE: ¼ inch cubes
   - Mirepoix, finely diced vegetables for quick cooking

4. BRUNOISE: ⅛ inch cubes (very fine dice)
   - Garnishes, fine sauces, elegant presentations

5. JULIENNE: thin matchsticks, ⅛ inch × ⅛ inch × 2-3 inches
   - Stir-fries, salads, garnishes

6. CHIFFONADE: thin ribbons of leafy herbs or greens
   - Stack leaves, roll tightly, slice thinly across the roll
   - Used for basil, mint, spinach

7. MINCE: very finely chopped, irregular, smaller than brunoise
   - Garlic, shallots, herbs

HOW TO JULIENNE:
1. Square off the vegetable — cut flat sides on all 4 sides
2. Slice into planks of desired thickness (⅛ inch)
3. Stack planks
4. Cut planks into sticks of same width

HOW TO DICE (from julienne):
1. Complete julienne cuts
2. Line up sticks
3. Cut across the sticks at the same interval as the stick width
4. Result: uniform cubes

KNIFE MAINTENANCE:
- Hone with a honing steel before each use — realigns the edge
- Sharpen with a whetstone every few months — removes metal to create a new edge
- A sharp knife is safer than a dull one — requires less force
- Wash by hand and dry immediately — dishwashers dull and damage blades

BOARD SAFETY:
- Place a damp towel under the cutting board to prevent sliding
- Use a board large enough — crowded boards cause accidents
